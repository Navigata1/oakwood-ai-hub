The Oakwood AI Story

A 2-minute 30-second cinematic brief for church leadership, community partners, and social media distribution.
Format: 16:9 Landscape + 9:16 Portrait cut
Resolution: 4K / 3840x2160
Duration: 2:30 (150 seconds)
Tone: Cinematic, hopeful, urgent
Video Timeline — 150 Seconds Total
0:00 Hook 0:15 Problem 0:45 Solution 1:30 Proof 2:00 Close 2:30
Scene 01 The Hook
0:00 — 0:15 (15 seconds)
Narration Script
"In 1975, schools banned calculators."

[pause — 2 beats]

"They were wrong."

[beat — visual transition]

"AI is the calculator of this generation."
TEXT OVERLAY: "86% of your students are already using AI."
Audio Direction
Open on silence. First line delivered slowly, weight on each word. "They were wrong" punches — slight echo/reverb. Music swells underneath from the third line forward. Suggested track: cinematic orchestral, tempo build.
Visual Direction
  • OPEN: Black frame. Slow fade-in of a vintage calculator — warm, sepia-toned, slightly blurred edges
  • CUT: The calculator screen reads "ERROR" — flash to white
  • TRANSITION: The white morphs into a glowing AI chat interface
  • TEXT ANIMATION: "86% of your students are already using AI." — builds word by word in gold text on dark background
  • HOLD on text for 2 full seconds before cutting
B-Roll Notes
Stock: vintage calculator, typewriter, school classroom circa 1970s. Can use AI-generated imagery for the morphing transition effect. Keep color grade: warm tones transitioning to cool blue-white.
Scene 02 The Problem
0:15 — 0:45 (30 seconds)
Narration Script
"Right now, your kids are learning AI on YouTube."

"Without guidance. Without values. Without you."

[pause]

"Only 6% of churches have an AI policy. 73% have nothing at all."

[beat]

"The gap is widening. Every single day."
GRAPHIC: 6% vs 94% — animated bar/donut chart in gold and dark tones
Audio Direction
Music becomes slightly discordant — tension builds. Each statistic lands with a subtle sonic "thud." Pacing is deliberate — do not rush these lines. Let the silence after "without you" breathe.
Visual Direction
  • OPEN: B-roll of a teenager on a phone, face lit by screen in a dark room
  • SPLIT SCREEN: phone screen showing YouTube AI tutorials vs. empty church meeting room
  • GRAPHIC: Animated stat card — "Only 6% of churches have AI policies" (Exponential.org, 2025)
  • GRAPHIC: Second stat — "73% have NO policy at all" — empty bar chart fills in with red
  • VISUAL: Time-lapse of a classroom clock, then a church bulletin board — no AI content visible
  • CLOSE: Wide shot of church sanctuary, empty. Feeling of missed opportunity.
B-Roll Notes
Avoid anything that looks accusatory. The tone is: "This isn't anyone's fault — the world moved fast." Suggest cool blue/grey color grading for this section to signal the problem phase.

0:45 — 1:30 (45 seconds)
Narration Script
"Introducing the Oakwood AI Initiative."

"A 12-week program. Three tracks. Every generation."

"From Pathfinders to elders — everyone learns together."

[beat — show the hub]

TEXT OVERLAY: "Zero cost. Zero barriers. Zero excuses." — animates in on white flash
Audio Direction
Music lifts here — triumphant, hopeful. Tempo increases slightly. The word "nothing" should hit with warmth, not harshness. Consider a brief musical swell on "nothing."
Visual Direction
  • OPEN: Screen recording of oakwood-ai-hub.vercel.app loading — smooth, impressive
  • QUICK CUTS: Hub landing page, /church, /academy, /pathfinders routes — 2 seconds each
  • TITLE CARDS: "Track 01: Foundations" / "Track 02: Creators" / "Track 03: Ministry" — elegant, gold text on dark
  • SPLIT SCREEN: Jon and James — side by side, professional portrait style
  • MONTAGE: Mixed age group learning — child on tablet, professional at laptop, elder in conversation
  • GRAPHIC: "$0 Cost to the Church" — large, gold, centered. Hold 2 seconds.
B-Roll Notes
This is the warmth section. Color grade: warm golds and ambers. Show real spaces — the CHAC auditorium, classroom settings. Use the actual hub website in screen recordings. Authentic over polished.
Scene 04 The Proof
1:30 — 2:00 (30 seconds)
Narration Script
"A 10-year-old built a working app in our first test session."

"A 70-year-old learned to manage health records with AI."

[beat — demo roll]

"Build an app in 60 seconds."

"Write a grant proposal for the church."

"Redesign a website without a developer."

"If you can have a conversation, you can learn this."
LOWER THIRD: "91% of church leaders support AI education. 87% are willing to invest." — Exponential.org 2025
Audio Direction
Upbeat momentum. Music is confident, almost playful. Each "demo item" should have a quick sonic cue — like a notification sound or a soft keyboard click. Final line "if you can have a conversation" should slow down slightly — warm and inviting.
Visual Direction
  • RAPID DEMO CUTS: Screen recordings — each demo 4-5 seconds max, fast-paced montage
  • DEMO 1: AI coding tool generating an app from a text prompt — show the result loading
  • DEMO 2: AI writing a church grant proposal in real-time — text streaming on screen
  • DEMO 3: Before/after — basic church website transforms into a polished design
  • DEMO 4: Sign-up form submission instantly appearing in Google Sheets
  • INTERCUT: Reaction shots — genuine surprise, delight, engagement from participants
  • GRAPHIC: Stat card fades in during final line — lower third style, subtle
B-Roll Notes
Screen recordings are key. Use real tools — Claude, Cursor, or similar. The 10-year-old and 70-year-old references should be illustrated, not literally shown (privacy). Consider animated "story cards" as visual stand-ins.
Scene 05 The Close
2:00 — 2:30 (30 seconds)
Narration Script
[slow, measured — scripture voice]

"Where there is no vision..."

"...the people perish."

[pause — 3 full seconds of silence with visual]

"Oakwood has vision."

"Join us."

[beat]

"oakwood-ai-hub.vercel.app"
FULL SCREEN TEXT: "Proverbs 29:18 — KJV" — white on black, hold 3 seconds
Audio Direction
Music drops to near-silence for the scripture. Single piano note or choir chord underneath. Builds slowly for "Oakwood has vision." Full swell for "Join us." URL reads clearly over music — do not let it get buried. Final 5 seconds: music out, URL displayed in silence.
Visual Direction
  • OPEN: Slow aerial or wide establishing shot of Oakwood campus — golden hour preferred
  • TITLE CARD: Scripture text builds word by word — "Where there is no vision..." in white on black
  • CUT TO: "...the people perish." — isolated, full screen, pause
  • TRANSITION: Slow dissolve to warm shot of community gathering — multi-generational
  • CLOSE: Oakwood AI Initiative logo mark, then URL
  • FINAL FRAME: Dark navy background, gold text — "oakwood-ai-hub.vercel.app" — centered, breathing room, QR code in corner
B-Roll Notes
The final frame must be screenshottable for sharing. High contrast, URL large and clear. Include the QR code in the bottom-right corner at approximately 15% of frame width. Hold for minimum 5 seconds.

Technical Production Notes

Primary Format
16:9 landscape, 3840x2160 (4K). Export H.264 MP4 for web; ProRes for editing master.
Social Cut (9:16)
Reframe for vertical — 1080x1920. Key text must remain within safe zone (80% of frame). Ideal for Instagram Reels, TikTok, WhatsApp Status.
Square Cut (1:1)
1080x1080 for Facebook / Instagram feed. Create separately from 16:9 master — do not auto-crop.
Color Grade
Problem section: desaturated cool blue. Solution section: warm gold and amber. Close: deep navy with high-contrast gold. Consistent with brand palette #1a2332 / #c9a84c.
Typography
All text overlays: Outfit font, weights 700-900. Gold (#c9a84c) on dark. White on very dark or black backgrounds only. No thin weights for text overlays.
Music
Royalty-free cinematic track. Tempo: ~80-100 BPM. Genre: orchestral-electronic hybrid. Suggested: Artlist.io or Epidemic Sound "inspiring documentary" category.
Captions
Auto-generate via Whisper AI, then proof manually. Captions required for accessibility and muted autoplay on social. Style: white text, black outline, bottom-center.
Screen Recordings
Record at 2x or 3x scale for crisp playback. Use a clean browser profile — no personal bookmarks or extensions visible. Disable notifications before recording.
Pacing
Hook: slow and deliberate. Problem: measured tension. Solution: confident flow. Proof: rapid montage. Close: slow and reverent. Do not rush scripture. Let silences breathe.
