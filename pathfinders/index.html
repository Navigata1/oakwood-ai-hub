The Moment We're In

86% of students already use AI.
Who's teaching them how?

According to the Digital Education Council, the vast majority of students are already using AI in their learning — with or without guidance. Usage surged from 66% in 2024 to 92% in just one year. The question isn't whether our Crusaders will use AI. It's whether they'll learn to use it here, with values, with purpose, with a Master Guide by their side — or figure it out alone on YouTube.

"The parents who ban AI are not protecting their children. They're delaying the inevitable — and their kids will learn it without guidance, without values, and without the foundation to use it wisely."

Pathfinders have always met the moment. When the world needed young people who could build a fire from scratch, we taught them. When it needed young people who could lead with integrity, we trained them. Now the world needs young people who can direct intelligence rather than be directed by it. That's not a tech problem. That's a Pathfinder problem.

The Pathfinder Way

Physical. Mental. Spiritual.
Now Digital.

The Pathfinder philosophy has always been built on a tripod: physical development, mental growth, and spiritual grounding. AI doesn't break that tripod — it extends it.

When a Crusader learns to specify a task precisely for an AI, they're practicing the same decomposition skills they use to plan a camp layout. When they evaluate AI output for accuracy, they're exercising the critical thinking we've always cultivated. When they build something that serves others, that's ministry through modern tools.

We don't replace the foundation. We build on it. Fundamentals first — always. Then we add the AI exoskeleton that extends what they can do.

The AI Pathway

Mapped to the Pathfinder progressive class structure — from Friend to Master Guide. Build the brain first, then add the tools.

What AI is and isn't. Critical thinking basics. How to evaluate information. Reading, writing, arithmetic — by hand first. Build the cognitive foundation.
