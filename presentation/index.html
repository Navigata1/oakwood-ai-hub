12-Week Framework

Curriculum Tracks

Three tracks, one philosophy: start with conversation, build through demonstration, sustain through community. Every session produces something tangible.

"What are your challenges? What interests you about AI? What scares you about it? Let's start there."

— Teaching philosophy: meet people where they are
Track 1 · Weeks 1–4

AI Foundations

"What is this thing and why should I care?"
WK 1

What AI Actually Is

Not magic, not evil, not new. A tool. Everyone asks AI a personal question live.

WK 2

AI in Daily Life

Health, finances, meal planning, family. Each person solves one real task with AI.

WK 3

AI for Communication

Emails, resumes, proposals, grant writing. Live: write a grant proposal in 5 minutes.

WK 4

AI Safety & Ethics

What to share, what not to, how to verify. Everyone tries to "break" AI — builds healthy skepticism.

Track 2 · Weeks 5–8

Creators & Entrepreneurs

"Now let's build something."
WK 5

AI for Business Ideas

Market research, validation, business plans. Validate an idea in 10 minutes live.

WK 6

Building Without Code

Websites, apps, landing pages. Build a site from scratch using Replit or Lovable.

WK 7

AI for Marketing

Social media, branding, content. Create a real campaign for their church or business.

WK 8

Voice Agents & Automation

Phone agents, workflows, CRM. Group project: build an agent for a local business.

Track 3 · Weeks 9–12

Ministry & Community

"How does this serve something bigger?"
WK 9

AI for Sermon & Study Prep

Research, outlines, cross-referencing. Prep a Bible study topic with AI assistance.

WK 10

AI for Outreach

Event planning, volunteer coordination. Plan a real upcoming church event together.

WK 11

AI for Education

Tutoring, study plans, career development. Students and parents build personalized plans.

WK 12

Showcase & Next Steps

Community presents what they've built. Vote on what to learn next. The cycle continues.

AI for the Community — Program Summary

What
A community AI literacy program open to all ages — church members, surrounding SDA congregations, and the greater Huntsville community. Hands-on, values-grounded, practical education.
Why
AI is reshaping every industry, career, and daily task. The digital divide is growing. Our community leads — we don't follow. This program ensures no one is left behind while the world rewrites itself.
Who Teaches
Jonathan Isaac (Island Development Crew LLC) — AI practitioner, software developer, community member with 15+ applications across 9 domains. Partnership under the IT Department's direction.
Who Attends
Everyone. Youth through Pathfinder integration, college students, professionals, entrepreneurs, and elders. No prior experience required. Surrounding churches and community welcome.
Schedule
Saturday evenings (after sunset, 7:00–8:30 PM), Sundays during Pathfinder hours, and bi-weekly weekday deep-dives. Sabbath-honoring in all scheduling.
Cost
$0 to the church. Instructor volunteers time. Uses existing church AV equipment. Attendees bring personal devices (phone is sufficient).
Revenue
YouTube content channel ("Oakwood IT Presents"), future course licensing opportunities, community partnerships, and eligibility for digital literacy grants.
Ownership
Church initiative owns the program brand and identity. Instructor retains rights to personal presentation content for educational use and distribution.

Bottom line: Zero cost. Immediate community impact. Passive revenue potential. Positions Oakwood as a leader in faith-based technology education. The only question is how fast we want to start.
